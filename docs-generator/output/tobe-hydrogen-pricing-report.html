SECTION 01

Why Hydrogen Prices Are So Confusing

Hydrogen pricing is one of the most opaque markets in the energy industry. When someone says "hydrogen costs $X per kilogram," that number could mean wildly different things depending on what's included:

  • Stack-only vs. system-level efficiency — Some producers quote the theoretical efficiency of just the electrolysis stack, ignoring power electronics, cooling, water treatment, compression, and purification. This is like quoting the fuel cost of an engine without the drivetrain.
  • Electricity price assumptions — The single biggest variable. Some analyses assume $0.02/kWh (available basically nowhere in practice), others use $0.06–0.08/kWh. A $0.01/kWh difference at 50 kWh/kg changes the cost by $0.50/kg.
  • Delivery terms — Is this hydrogen at the plant gate? Compressed to 200 bar? Liquefied? Delivered 50 miles? The delivery, compression, and logistics are often 75–85% of the final delivered cost (per DOE).
  • Margin — Some prices are breakeven costs, some include 15% margin, some 30%. Some don't say which.
  • Subsidies — Is the 45V production tax credit ($3/kg) included? LCFS credits? Carbon credits? Some quoted prices bake in subsidies that may not exist in 5 years.
  • Scale — A 1 MW pilot and a 100 MW facility have fundamentally different economics. Some prices reflect theoretical scale that doesn't exist yet.
THE RESULT

An investor or customer reads five different hydrogen cost claims and has no way to compare them. Everyone sounds cheap when you get to pick your own assumptions.
