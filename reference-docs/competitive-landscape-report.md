> **Date:** March 2026

## Executive Summary

The green hydrogen space has attracted billions in venture capital, but most funding has gone to companies iterating on conventional electrolysis architectures — PEM, alkaline, and SOEC. A smaller cohort of "non-conventional" startups claims breakthrough approaches. This report benchmarks Tobe Energy against the three most comparable non-conventional electrolysis startups: **Advanced Ionics**, **Fourier**, and **HGen**.

**The bottom line:** Tobe Energy achieved a more advanced pilot system than all three comparables — with less capital, fewer employees, and full in-house engineering and manufacturing capability. While competitors are still scaling lab prototypes, Tobe is deploying commercially.

## Company Profiles

### Advanced Ionics (Milwaukee, WI)
- **Founded:** 2017 (8 years old)
- **Employees:** 26
- **Total raised:** $21.1M (Seed + $16.7M Series A, Nov 2024)
- **Pre-money valuation:** $42.3M (Series A)
- **Lead investors:** bp Ventures, Mitsubishi Heavy Industries
- **Technology:** RSOEC-like vapor-phase electrolysis ("Symbion" electrolyzer)
- **Operating temp:** ~300°C (requires thermal management infrastructure)
- **Membraneless:** Yes (vapor-phase eliminates traditional membrane)
- **Rare earths:** Uses nickel and stainless steel (no platinum/iridium)
- **Efficiency claim:** 35 kWh/kg target (stack only, not yet demonstrated at system level; ~75% HHV estimated including waste heat)
- **Pilot scale:** Small — 800 cm² active area
- **Pilot cost:** ~$4M (thermal integration and custom enclosure)
- **Manufacturing:** Partnered scale-up (not in-house)
- **Engineering:** Stack only — power electronics and BOP sourced externally
- **Status:** Pre-revenue, R&D stage. Series A funds allocated to facility buildout and development acceleration.

**Key takeaway:** Founded in 2017 with $21M raised and 26 employees over 8 years. Still at 800 cm² lab scale. The vapor-phase approach has theoretical thermodynamic advantages but requires high operating temperatures (300°C) and adds thermal management complexity that offsets efficiency gains at the system level.

### Fourier (San Francisco, CA)
- **Founded:** 2021
- **Employees:** 5
- **Total raised:** $23.6M (Seed + $18.5M Series A-1/A-2 + debt, Dec 2024)
- **Pre-money valuation:** $62M (Series A)
- **Lead investors:** Airbus Ventures, General Catalyst, Paramark Ventures
- **Other investors:** Borusan Ventures, Positive Ventures, GSBackers, MCJ
- **Technology:** PEM stack (conventional architecture, "modular blade" form factor)
- **Membraneless:** No — uses standard PEM membrane
- **Rare earths:** Yes — platinum, IrO₂ (iridium oxide)
- **Efficiency:** 50-55% HHV (standard PEM range)
- **Pilot scale:** Small — modular blade unit
- **Pilot cost:** ~$5M
- **Manufacturing:** Assembly only (components sourced externally)
- **Engineering:** Off-the-shelf PEM stack integration
- **Status:** Listed as "Generating Revenue" on PitchBook (likely small pilot sales/grants)

**Key takeaway:** Despite the "non-conventional" positioning, Fourier is essentially a conventional PEM electrolyzer repackaged in a modular form factor. Still requires platinum, iridium, and membranes. The $62M pre-money valuation for 5 employees and a small modular PEM system reflects the frothy end of cleantech venture pricing. Raised nearly $24M total to build what is fundamentally one upgrade away from being a standard commercial PEM system.

### HGen (San Francisco, CA)
- **Founded:** 2021
- **Employees:** 6
- **Total raised:** $7M (Seed, Sep 2024)
- **Pre-money valuation:** $20M
- **Lead investor:** Seven Seven Six (Alexis Ohanian)
- **Other investors:** Founders Fund, Fontinalis Partners
- **Technology:** Modular electrolyzer (details limited; positioned as "decarbonizing steel and ammonia")
- **Status:** Pre-revenue, early development. Funds for commercial deployment acceleration.

**Key takeaway:** Earliest stage of the comparables. High-profile investors (Founders Fund, Ohanian) but limited public technical disclosure. $20M valuation on $5M seed with 6 employees.

## What This Means

### 1. Capital Efficiency
Tobe built a 6,400 cm² pilot system for $300K. Advanced Ionics spent $4M for an 800 cm² system. Fourier spent $5M for a small modular blade. On a per-cm² basis:

| Company | Pilot cost | Active area | $/cm² |
|---------|-----------|-------------|-------|
| **Tobe** | **$300K** | **6,400 cm²** | **$47/cm²** |
| Advanced Ionics | $4M | 800 cm² | $5,000/cm² |
| Fourier | $5M | Small (est. ~500 cm²) | ~$10,000/cm² |

Tobe is **100x more capital-efficient** at the pilot stage. This isn't an incremental advantage — it's a structural one that comes from vertical integration and in-house engineering.

### 2. Speed of Execution
- Advanced Ionics was founded in **2017** and is still at lab scale after 8 years and $21M.
- Fourier was founded in **2021** and has a small modular PEM unit after 4 years and $24M.
- Tobe was founded in **2024** and is deploying a **600kW commercial system** within 2 years of founding on $1.8M.

### 3. Technology Differentiation
Advanced Ionics' vapor-phase approach eliminates the membrane but introduces a 300°C operating temperature — requiring insulation, thermal management, and high-temperature materials that add cost and complexity. Fourier isn't really non-conventional at all — it's a PEM electrolyzer in a modular package, still dependent on platinum, iridium, and membranes.

Tobe operates at <30°C with no membrane, no rare earths, and no cooling system. The simplicity of the architecture is the competitive advantage.

### 4. Pricing Transparency
Tobe is the only company in this cohort to publish its hydrogen pricing methodology through an independent third party (S&P Platts). At $7.05/kg delivered with 30% margin and no subsidies, Tobe's pricing is verifiable and competitive with conventional hydrogen delivery.

## What NOT to Say

- Don't claim these companies will fail — they have serious backers and may succeed
- Don't overstate efficiency where their tech has genuinely different physics (vapor-phase thermodynamics)
- Don't mock their investors — bp, Airbus, General Catalyst, Founders Fund are serious firms
- DO let the numbers speak for themselves — the capital efficiency gap is the story
- DO emphasize deployment timeline — commercial production is the only metric that ultimately matters
