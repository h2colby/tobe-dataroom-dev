# Why Hydrogen Prices Are So Confusing — And How Tobe Cuts Through the Noise

## The Problem: Nobody Is Comparing Apples to Apples

Hydrogen pricing is one of the most opaque markets in the energy industry. When someone says "hydrogen costs $X per kilogram," that number could mean wildly different things depending on what's included:

- **Stack-only efficiency** vs. **system-level efficiency** — Some producers quote the theoretical efficiency of just the electrolysis stack, ignoring the power electronics, cooling, water treatment, compression, and purification that actually make up the real cost. This is like quoting the fuel cost of an engine without the drivetrain.

- **Electricity price assumptions** — The single biggest variable. Some analyses assume $0.02/kWh (available basically nowhere in practice), others use $0.06-0.08/kWh (closer to reality). A $0.01/kWh difference at 50 kWh/kg changes the cost by $0.50/kg.

- **Delivery terms** — Is this hydrogen at the plant gate? Compressed to 200 bar in a tube trailer? Liquefied? Delivered 50 miles? 200 miles? The delivery, compression, and logistics are often **75-85% of the final delivered cost** (per DOE). A $2/kg production cost easily becomes $15-25/kg by the time it reaches the customer.

- **Margin** — Some prices are breakeven costs, some include a 15% margin, some include 30%. Some don't say which.

- **Subsidies** — Is the 45V production tax credit ($3/kg) included? LCFS credits? Carbon credits? Some quoted prices bake in subsidies that may or may not exist in 5 years.

- **Scale** — A 1 MW pilot plant and a 100 MW facility have fundamentally different economics. Some prices reflect theoretical scale that doesn't exist yet.

The result: an investor or customer reads five different hydrogen cost claims and has no way to compare them. Everyone sounds cheap when you get to pick your own assumptions.

## How Tobe Solves This: Published Methodology via S&P Platts

Rather than argue about assumptions, we submitted our full cost methodology to **S&P Global Commodity Insights (Platts)** — the same organization that sets benchmark prices for oil, gas, and commodities worldwide. Platts published our pricing in their Hydrogen Price Index, making us one of the first small-scale electrolysis companies to have an independent, published benchmark.

Our Platts submission is fully transparent. Every assumption is documented:

## Where Tobe Sits

**Without any subsidies:** Tobe's published Platts pricing is $7.05/kg delivered (50 miles, 30% margin). That's competitive with Plug Power's **pickup-only** price for **conventional** hydrogen — and we're delivering green hydrogen with a profit margin built in.

**With 45V ($3/kg credit):** Our effective delivered price drops to ~$4.05/kg with margin. At that price, we undercut virtually every non-pipeline hydrogen source in the country.

**The real comparison:** The typical industrial customer buying hydrogen by the truckload is paying $8-15/kg today. We're offering $7.05 green, delivered, with margin — or $4.05 with 45V. That's not just competitive. That's disruptive.
